Instructions

We are evaluating a system which automatically extracts cause-and-effect pairs from text.

A cause is something which directly results in an effect.

You will be given a 2-3 sentence passage to read and a pair of phrases extracted from the passage. Your task will be to determine if the pair of phrases are

  1. Causal: After reading the passage, you can conclude that the extracted cause DOES result in the extracted effect.
  2. Not Causal: After reading the passage, you can conclude that the extracted cause does NOT result in the extracted effect.

Let's take a look at the following example. We have this passage with the intended cause highlighted in orange and the intended effect highlighted in blue.

Temperatures and pressures at the center of the cloud were extreme. It was so hot that nuclear fusion reactions began. In these reactions hydrogen fuses to make helium.

The above example would be labeled Causal because the possible cause (it being hot) results in the possible effect (the nuclear fusion reaction beginning).

The following example, on the other hand, would be labeled Not Causal

Sediments are different sizes of rock particles. Boulders are sediments; so is gravel. At the other end, silt and clay are also sediments.

This example is Not Causal because boulders being sediments does NOT directly result in gravel being sediments. These are two independent facts that are not causally related.
